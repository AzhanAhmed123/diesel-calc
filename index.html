Environmental Impact & Mitigation

The Hidden Cost of Our Power Backup

In Karachi, the hum of a diesel generator is a sound we all know too well. For every single liter of diesel consumed, nearly 2.7 kilograms of CO₂ is pumped into our local air.

These generators also release "particulate matter"—tiny soot particles that make the air heavy and harmful to breathe, especially for children in crowded urban areas.

Turning Karachi’s Heat into Help

Our city receives over five hours of peak sunlight daily. By capturing this energy with Solar PV panels, we can "turn off" generators during the day.

This "Hybrid" approach drastically reduces pollution and creates an economic shield against rising fuel prices in Pakistan.
